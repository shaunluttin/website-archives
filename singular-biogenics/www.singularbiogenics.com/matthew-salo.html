Matthew Salo

Overview

Singular Biogenics Corporation principal, Matthew Salo, was born in Seattle and raised in Arcata, California, in the relatively remote Redwood region of the Pacific coast. Constant interaction with an astounding natural world and attendance at College Elementary School, an alternative school on the campus of Humboldt State University (HSU) emphasizing creativity with low student/teacher ratios and no academic grades developed a love of nature and a creative confidence in abilities. He was strongly influenced by the environmental focus of his father’s work as Chairman of the Division of Natural Resources at HSU.

Moving back to the Seattle area, his natural inclination for problem solving fueled interest in science and math at high school and university. Although having a propensity for academics and problem solving, he resisted a standard university education.  He became an avid mountaineer in the surrounding Cascades and Olympics and acquired SCUBA certification at age 16. He developed means to balance his love of the outdoors with his talent for logic by working as a marine research technician on Puget Sound for the University of Washington (UW) and for an environmental company on the Beaufort Sea.  He was first exposed to the importance of algae culture at an experimental shellfish hatchery in the early Seventies and learned computer programming on the job at the UW in the late Seventies.  Eventually, he graduated from The Evergreen State College, a liberal arts school with an interdisciplinary approach and written evaluations in place of grades, extending his alternative education experience. Turning down an invitation to pursue post-grad studies at Stanford Artificial Intelligence Laboratory, he lived in Japan for seven years, working for Fujitsu before moving to Victoria, British Columbia,  as Vice President Systems Development for a high tech startup and then nearby Salt Spring Island  to begin a mussel aquaculture venture.

Salo has demonstrated an attraction to innovative projects including founding the www.mls.ca website, Salt Spring Island mussels, Bruce's Kitchen, a local food restaurant and Heart and Bone Provisions supporting the ketogenic diet. The focus of his interest centers on food and energy production while minimizing impact on the environment. Salo has a lifelong commitment to live in responsible and compassionate relationship with self, society and the environment.
