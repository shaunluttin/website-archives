How do I streamline my access to Yahoo Email?

Friday, February 28, 2014 6:06:00 PM

Summary

We fixed Internet Explorer by reseting it's options through the control panel. We also tried to speed up the computer by disabled startup programs AND by disabled visual effects. Here are some more details. 

Details

  • Why am I not connected to the Internet?
  • Your computer was using your Home Wireless Network. Not that you are not at home, your computer needs to connect to another network. This other network can be either wired or wireless. Solution: connect to a network.
  • Why does my Internet Explorer stay blank forever when I open it?
  • When the browser first opens, it goes to its homepage. Internet Explorer on your computer does not have a home page, so it doesn't go anywhere, it just stay blank.
  • Solution: set a homepage. Actually, that didn't work :-( because the "Internet Options" menu item is grayed out (i.e. it is disabled). 
  • Why is Internet Options in Internet Explorer grayed out? 
  • We don't know. That's odd. It might be a virus that has changed your group policy. What does that mean? Well, it means that your computer might have a virus.
  • Action: scan the computer for a virus. Done. Avast did not find any threats.
  • Action: reset Internet Explorer settings. Done. We did this through Control Panel > Internet Options > Advanced. This fixed it!
  • Why is the computer running so slow? There are lots of ways to speed up a computer. Here is a list that Microsoft suggests to try: http://windows.microsoft.com/en-ca/windows/optimize-windows-better-performance#optimize-windows-better-performance=windows-vista
  • Delete programs you never use.
  • Limit how many programs run at startup. Go to Start > Control Panel > Administrative Tools > System Configuration > Startup. Uncheck any program that you do NOT want to run on starting your computer. When in double, probably disabled it.
  • Defragment your hard drive.
  • Clean up your hard disk
  • Run fewer programs at the same time
  • Turn off visual effects. Go to Start > Control Panel > Performance Info and Tools > Advanced Tools > Adjust the Appearance and Performance of Windows > Adjust for best performance.
  • Try restarting if there's a problem
  • Add more memory
  • Check for viruses and spyware
  • Check your computer’s speed
  • Check for updates from the manufacturer
