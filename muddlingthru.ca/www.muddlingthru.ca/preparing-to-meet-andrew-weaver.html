Preparing to Meet Andrew Weaver

Tags: career, climate-change

Wednesday, September 17, 2014 5:49:18 PM

Today I am meeting Andrew Weaver, MLA.

  • http://climate.uvic.ca/people/weaver/
  • http://www.andrewweavermla.ca/

These are some questions that I might ask if I were meeting me. While Andrew might not have the same questions, it’s a reasonable way to prepare.

Why are you here?

  • I would like to contribute my technical skills toward climate change mitigation and adaptation.
  • I want an increased understanding of how climate change will impact the world over the next 30 years.

How can I help you?

  • You can help me to find a fit between my skills and the important problems in climate change.
  • If there’s a fit that’s appropriate for a master’s program at UVIC, then you can help me with both the application process and funding.

What are your skills?

  • I have five years of experience in software development for business processes, websites, and automation.
  • I have strong abstract problem solving and communication skills.
  • I like to spend a long time incrementally solving hard problems.

What type of problems most interest you? What’s your preferred type of work?

  • Well, I’m more introverted,
  • more scientific/technical than artistic/subjective,
  • more interested in working independently than part of a highly interactive or micro managed team,
  • more inclined toward concrete goals than vague or nebulous finish lines, and
  • driven more to do something than to persuade or manage a group.
  • I’m very persistent and I love to learn.
  • I like to work on important problems that have technical solutions. I like open source. I like community involvement.

Okay, so what are some example problems you have considered?

  • How to use software and robotics to optimize the energy collection of residential solar panels and to feed it back into the grid of small communities.
  • How to use environmental monitoring, software, and robotics to increase the yield of small, residential permaculture while minimizing water usage.
  • How to motivate individuals to engage in more sustainable living practices.

What would it look like if you got what you really wanted?

  • I would receive two years of funding to solve an important climate change related problem that is amenable to a software/robotics solution.
  • At the end of the two years, I would have solved the problem and rolled out the solution in a small community such as Salt Spring Island.
  • The foundation will have been laid to roll out the solution in other small communities.
