High-Density Algae Summary

  

April 2014

Business Model

In 2010, Singular Biogenics Corporation (SBC) began exploration of the development of technology to grow microalgae at ultra-high density.  A literature survey conducted by SBC provided an in-depth understanding of factors influencing autotrophic production and revealed the necessary criteria to produce algae two to four orders of magnitude denser than produced by systems currently on the market.  Technology implementing these criteria is now under development by SBC that will produce these high densities at commercial scale.  Over the course of three years, a small manufacturing/assembly facility can be built and sales and client support initiated.  Unlike many algae initiatives that are not based on true technological advancement but speculate on the various uses of algae, this business model is the result of a sustained effort developing production technology founded on principles outlined in peer-reviewed research and offers a technological advancement producing algal densities greater than any autotrophic system on the market today.

As the result of SBC’s principal’s experience in marine biological research and shellfish aquaculture in British Columbia and Washington state, the initial market for the technology is shellfish hatcheries which culture autotrophic microalgae as live feed for bivalve larvae.  Dr. Helen Gurney-Smith of the Centre for Shellfish Research at Vancouver Island University stated SBC’s technology will revolutionize the hatchery-based shellfish industry.  It is believed the technology with its very small footprint and automated functions will be in demand to the established and growing worldwide hatchery-based shellfish and marine finfish industries. 

However, recent interest in microalgae as 1) the most sustainable source of a variety of biogenic substances of increasing demand such as omega-3 and -6 oils, antioxidants, proteins, anti-cancer pharmaceuticals and other valuable substances, 2) a means to convert industrial carbon emissions to these substances and 3) realization that microalgae culture is the most efficient and environmentally responsible way to produce photosynthetic fuel oil has created a rapidly expanding market to algal research and development efforts.  The automated data-rich modular technology would be of interest to companies researching and developing algae-based cosmetics, nutraceuticals or pharmaceuticals.  

In addition to technology sales, one or more defined efforts could be launched by SBC or associated enterprises that use the technology to enhance sustainable food production, healthy plant-based biogenic substance production and/or mitigation of industrial carbon and nitrogen emissions.  For example, a large and efficient mussel aquaculture enterprise could be established in British Columbia that uses this algae production technology.  It is possible for SBC to enter a partnership with a brewpub to test the commercial prototype at the brewery while developing techniques to mitigate carbon dioxide emissions from the fermentation processes.  Also, SBC is discussing the technology with the National Research Council Canada for use in a collaborative relationship with their flagship Algal Carbon Conversion initiative to convert industry-emitted carbon dioxide to valuable biogenics.

Management Team

SBC’s management is committed to the development of the high-density algae production technology and to using the technology for social benefit through improving human diets and re-using industrial waste.  Above all, SBC is dedicated to operating responsibly in relationship to team members, clients, society and the environment.  SBC was formed as a for-profit corporation but an alternative business structure that explicitly expresses the mission and values of the management team, such as a Social Enterprise, will be formed and ownership of the technology will be transferred to the new corporation.

Finances

SBC has completed a research-phase system to gain understanding of capabilities of system design and components.  Based on knowledge gained in this investigative phase of development, SBC is seeking $150,000 in investments and grants over one year to develop a commercial prototype and apply for patents.  A basic prototype will be completed in six months with an additional year and a half of laboratory and client testing and refinements.  On completion, an additional $200,000 will be obtained to establish a small assembly facility and build sales beyond the breakeven point of four units per month.  The sales will support continuous operation of the R & D team to produce further improvements that will enhance the technology’s market position in addition to developing one or more of several defined opportunities for additional revenue streams.  SBC is actively pursuing funding partnerships for this work including NSERC support through a local college.
