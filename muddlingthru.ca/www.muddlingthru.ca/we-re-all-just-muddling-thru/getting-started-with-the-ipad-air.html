Getting Started with the iPad Air

Friday, January 10, 2014 6:07:00 PM

General Tips

  • Avoid trying to make your iPad interact with your Microsoft technologies, because this is advanced. It will lead to great frustration. In general, Apple, Microsoft, and Google product do not cooperate well.

Navigation

In general, there are the following ways to interact with an iPad screen. 

  1. Tap. This is similar to a mouse click. 
  2. Double Tap. This is similar to a double click. 
  3. Tap and Hold. This is usually to access more advanced functionality and is similar to a right click in the Windows operating system.
  4. Swipe. The swipe can be up, down, left or right and is for directional navigation. 
  5. Pinch. Zoom in on the screen.
  6. Starburst. Zoom out on the screen. 

Also, there are two ways to interact with the iPad buttons.

  1. Press. 
  2. Press and Hold. This is usually for less common activities like reseting the iPad. 

Homework

Write down your AppleID and Password, because these are required to access some iPad features.

  • Once we have this, the next step is to setup the iPad with your AppleID.
  • This isn't required but it is necessary for using features such as the App Store. 

Make a list of things that you want to do with the iPad, because the way to make progress is to focus your energy. 

  • Checking email. 
  • Sending email. 
  • Replying to email. 
  • Attaching images to email. 
  • Accessing music through iTunes. 
  • Surf the Internet using a browser (okay... what exactly do you want to accomplish on the Internet)?
  • Use a search engine to ask questions such as "what is the weather like today?" and to find exactly what you're looking for on the Internet. 
  • Convert voice to text (okay... what exactly do you want to narrate). 
  • Convert voice to text to compose emails. 
  • Use iPad Apps. 

Conduct a survey of friends and family to ask what their favorite uses of the iPad are. 

  • Check email. 
  • Go to my favorite websites. 
  • Look up words in the dictionary (e.g. with the Dictionary.com App).
  • Read the news on your favorite news source (e.g. CBC, HackerNews, The Guardian, The Huffington Post, Al Jazera).
  • Checkout e-books from the library.
  • Use Skype to comminicate with children and friends.
