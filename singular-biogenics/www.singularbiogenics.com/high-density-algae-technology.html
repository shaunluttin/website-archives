High-Density Algae Technology

In 2010, Singular Biogenics Corporation (SBC) began exploration of the development of technology to grow microalgae at ultra-high density with intention to use the technology in shellfish hatcheries. A literature survey conducted by SBC provided an in-depth understanding of factors influencing microalgae production and revealed the necessary criteria to produce algae at 10% algal density, two to four orders of magnitude denser than produced by systems currently on the market. Technology implementing these criteria is now under development by SBC that will produce these high densities at commercial scale.  The technology is modular providing replication and/or redundancy, suitable for both research or production facilities.

In order to control the defined factors and reach densities near the theoretical maximum, SBC has determined the system must implement a fairly strict environmental regimen using relatively sophisticated technology that includes these features:

•   Artificial lights must be used to provide continual energy input.

•   A very short light path (the maximum distance between any algae cell and the nearest light source) of 5 mm or less is required.

•   The wavelength of the light provided should be as close as possible to the absorption spectra of algae pigments to maximize energy usage and limit overheating.

•   A closed system is required rather than ponds or tanks open to the air in order that CO2 and O2 levels are efficiently maintained.

•   Rigorous system sterility must be insured to avoid bacterial infestation.

•   The effect of the self-excreted large-molecule growth inhibitors must be mitigated.

•   Controlled mixing of the culture solution is necessary to maintain balanced access to light and nutrients.

•   Cooling is required for both the algae solution and electronic equipment, particularly lamps.

•   Computer automation with sensor feedback must be used to facilitate system control.

•   A modular system of multiple reactors with central systems for liquids, gases, cooling and sterilization management can best meet production requirements while increasing overall efficiency and reliability.

To be useful to existing markets, the system is scalable; it can be made of smaller reactor units for culture of multiple species or strains suitable for research facilities or larger reactors for replicate cultures of the same or complementary species suitable for production facilities. The ultra-high densities obtained allow a compact system footprint. One unit of up to 10 smaller reactors measures about one cubic meter. Such a system provides all the production and variety needed for a research or small production facility. One or two larger reactor units, each measuring about two cubic meters, are adequate for a medium-sized production facility, replacing hundreds of square feet of interior space dedicated to conventional algae culture or acres of outdoor ponds. Complete automation of culture functions with extended, near-perpetual cultures create a system with extremely low labour requirements. Internet connectivity allows remote observation and operation. Data collection allows “Industrial Internet” functionality for system reliability.

Preliminary design and trials have been completed gaining understanding of capabilities of system components.  The next phase involves refinement of system perfusion, the creation of a prototype for commercial production and extensive trials.
