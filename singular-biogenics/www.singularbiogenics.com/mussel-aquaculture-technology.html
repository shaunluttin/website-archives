Mussel Aquaculture Technology

A very significant opportunity exists for developing a technology-based mussel aquaculture enterprise in British Columbia (BC).  SBC’s principal, Matthew Salo, has extensive experience developing mussel farming technology for BC waters. As a founding partner in Island Sea Farms, Inc., producer of Salt Spring Island Mussels, he built the first hatchery dedicated to mussels in North America and designed, built and operated six farms in different areas along the BC coast.  

Researching mussel culture techniques, Salo travelled to New Zealand’s Marlborough Sounds and experienced first-hand the advanced technology and automation used in NZ’s mussel aquaculture industry.  NZ’s technology-based system heavily influenced Salo’s subsequent design and operation of mussel growout in BC.  Growout technology imported from NZ and used in BC includes the double longline system, hook and davit rope lifting systems, continuous rope, Christmas tree and looped crop rope, declumpers, conveyors and large harvest bags.  Initially, NZ’s continuous sock seeding technology was imported and used, but was replaced by a twine setting and twine-crop rope twining system appropriate for hatchery seed developed and implemented with extraordinary success.  

SBC's high-density algae culture system now under development is an excellent example of ultra-efficient technology for mussel culture.  The creation of technology-based systems to maximize efficiency, many mirroring those used in NZ, is critical for successful and profitable mussel aquaculture in BC and is SBC’s hallmark.
